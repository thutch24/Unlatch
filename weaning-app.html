3-Week Plan

🎯 Week 1 Goal: Drop the Midday Nap Feed

This week is all about building a new sleep association first, then using it to replace the nap feed. Don't rush — the foundation you build this week makes everything easier later.

🌿 Week 2 Goal: Drop the Morning Feed

You dropped the nap feed — incredible. Now apply the same approach to mornings. Use your comfort object and ritual, unlatch while drowsy, and hold the new routine.

🌙 Week 3 Goal: Bedtime + Overnight

The hardest week, but you have momentum. Bedtime and overnight are connected — tackle them together. If possible, have your partner take nights this week. "Milk is sleeping. Mummy is here."

Tips & Strategies
When She's Screaming
🗣️
Your anchor phrase Say "Milk is sleeping. Mummy is here." calmly and consistently every single time. Repetition helps her brain form a new expectation.
🫂
You can still comfort her Go to her, hold her, pat her, lie next to her. You are NOT abandoning her — you're just not offering the boob. The relationship is still intact.
💪
Stay the course Nights 1–5 are the hardest. Behaviour escalates before it improves — that's completely normal and it means it's working.
Building a New Sleep Cue
🧸
Comfort object Introduce a small stuffed animal or blanket during feeds for a few days first, so it starts to feel familiar before any feeds are dropped.
🎵
Same song every time Even just humming. Pair the new cue with the drowsy feeling so her brain connects them.
😴
Unlatch while drowsy, not asleep Feed until she's drowsy but not completely out. Unlatch, keep holding, keep humming. Let her cross into sleep without the nipple. This is the key skill — once she has it, dropping feeds is far less traumatic.
Overnight Strategies
👨‍👩‍👧
Partner takes nights The single most effective shortcut. She's less likely to escalate the same way when the boob isn't physically in the room.
💧
Offer water Having a sippy cup of water available for night wakes gives you something to offer that isn't the boob.
📅
Gradual window method No feeds before 3am for a few nights, then push to 5am, then morning only. Takes 1–2 weeks but is gentler.
For Your Body
🥬
Cabbage leaves Genuinely help with engorgement discomfort. Pop in the fridge first for extra relief.
🤲
Hand express minimally If engorged, express just enough for comfort — not to full relief. This signals your body to reduce supply.
Fussy Eating During Weaning
🍽️
Shift the dinner order Try: dinner → bath → books → feed → sleep. The gap between dinner and the feed means food has time to register and she arrives less ravenous.
🧀
Healthy fats and protein keep her fuller Cheese, avocado, egg, meat. Don't stress about variety right now — just get calories in.
😐
Ignore the rejection Neutral face, no reaction, clear the plate without comment. Food refusal is often a control game — removing the reaction removes the reward.
📈
Food will improve after weaning Once the boob is less available, food becomes more interesting by default. Most toddlers start eating more at dinner within 1–2 weeks of the bedtime feed dropping.
Preparing Your Toddler
💬
Tell her during the day Simple language: "Mummy's milk is going to sleep soon." Toddlers understand more than we think, and a heads-up helps.
❤️
Extra daytime cuddles Reassures her the relationship isn't going anywhere — just the milk. Connection is the antidote to anxiety.
You've got this 💛
18 months of breastfeeding is genuinely incredible. You are not abandoning her — you are teaching her a new way to feel safe. A few hard nights in exchange for consistent sleep is worth it, for both of you. She is almost 18 months old. She is ready, even if she doesn't think so yet.
